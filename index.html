Hi, my name is Maryla Wozniak.

I am an Institutional Research Analyst at Columbia University’s School of Engineering and Applied Science (SEAS), where I support strategic planning and executive decision-making through advanced analytics, automated reporting, and high-level data presentations. My work spans Python, SQL, Power BI, and Google Apps Script, and increasingly centers on developing efficient, scalable solutions that streamline workflows and improve data accuracy across the institution.

My background in data and programming began at Baruch College, where I transitioned from Finance to Computer Information Systems after discovering my passion for coding and data structures. Since then, I have built strong expertise in data manipulation, visualization, and automation, and I enjoy applying these skills to complex analytical challenges.

At SEAS, I work independently on a wide range of projects; from enrollment modeling and institutional reporting to dashboard development and long-term trend analysis. I value creativity, autonomy, and clarity in communicating insights, especially in presentations for executive-level audiences.

I am currently pursuing my Master of Science in Quantitative Methods in the Social Sciences (QMSS) at Columbia University, with an expected graduation in February 2027, further expanding my training in statistics, machine learning, and research methodology.

I am a highly analytical and detail-oriented data professional who loves to explore new ways of problem-solving. I believe my skills and experience make me a great fit for any team looking to tackle complex data challenges and drive data-driven decision-making.
